The sealed list is a launchpad. ↑↓ moves focus inside the sealed folder; Enter on a subfolder row blooms that subfolder OUT of its parent and into the graph as a new compound node, joined back by a synthetic edge. The parent folder stays sealed and updates its ×count badge to reflect the externalized child. This is the explicit answer to "the user has decided this branch matters — give it a node." Repeated blooms grow the graph organically without forcing the user to expand the whole tree at once.

contract

Enter on a subfolder is a graph mutation. The bloomed subfolder leaves its parent's list and lands in the canvas as a real compound node — recursively foldable, edge-bearing, draggable. The synthetic edge between parent and child is the provenance trace: it records that the child was promoted from this parent.

re-seal as undo

Shift+Enter on a bloomed compound node re-seals it back into the parent's list. This makes the bloom non-destructive and reversible, which is critical: novice users will bloom by accident, and they need a clean way back.

vs DRILL

DRILL never mutates the graph; SURFACE always does. This is the fork point in the design tree. Pick SURFACE if the graph IS the navigation map; pick DRILL if the graph is a stable canvas and the sealed list is a side-pane.
