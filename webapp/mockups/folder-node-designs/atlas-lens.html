contract

The graph data is invariant — what changes is the camera. Lens mode is to drilling what zoom-to-folder is to selecting-in-finder: you don't move files, you reframe your view of them. The viewport breadcrumb at the top is the URL; Esc is the back button.

scale superpower

Because the focused folder becomes the new canvas, you get the full canvas budget back at every zoom level. A folder with 600 children that would crush layout at the root can render comfortably as its own viewport. This is the strongest answer to BF-112.

cost

You lose peripheral context on zoom. Sibling regions and ancestor edges disappear from view. Mitigation: a small "minimap" inset in the corner showing the parent context, plus the persistent viewport breadcrumb. Acceptable trade for the canvas budget recovered.
