The user picked Atlas. Now: what does Enter do on a focused row inside the sealed folder's embedded file-tree? Four contracts below — each a different answer to the same interaction question. Pick one; the other three become recoverable as keyboard modifiers.

Drill — Enter pushes a breadcrumb and re-renders the list one level deeper. Graph untouched. Pure preview. Cheapest, safest.
Surface — Enter blooms the focused subfolder out of the sealed list and into the graph as a new compound node. Graph grows organically.
Lens — Enter relocates the camera. Focused folder becomes the new graph root; siblings disappear from view. Best answer to BF-112 scale.
Hybrid — All three available; mode selector in the control bar, per-folder defaults, modifier-key overrides. Most flexible, most surface area.
